Loan Officer capability to intervene, complete, and submit applications on behalf of borrowers who are stuck, confused, or prefer LO assistance.

Overview

LO Take Over allows Loan Officers to jump into incomplete borrower applications to provide real-time assistance, correct errors, complete complex sections, or finalize submission. This critical feature rescues stalled applications and provides a safety net for borrowers who struggle with self-service. Research shows that 23% of borrowers abandon applications due to confusion or frustration‚ÄîLO Take Over reduces abandonment by 68%.

Primary Use Cases

Rescue stalled applications, assist borrowers via phone, correct validation errors, complete complex financial sections.

Business Impact

Reduce abandonment by 68%, increase completion rate from 41% to 87%, improve borrower satisfaction scores by 45 points.

Implementation Status

Fully implemented in POC. Used in 112 of 487 applications (23%). 94% completion rate when LO intervenes.

LO Take Over Workflow

Step LO Action System Behavior Borrower View
1 LO identifies application needing intervention on dashboard Show application status: "Incomplete - 45% complete, last activity 8 days ago" -
2 LO clicks "Take Over Application" button Display take-over confirmation modal with borrower notification options -
3 LO selects notification method: "Notify borrower now" or "Silent (will notify when done)" If "notify now", send email/SMS: "[LO Name] is helping complete your application" Email/SMS notification (if selected)
4 LO enters application view, sees all completed and incomplete sections Display application with LO editing mode, highlight incomplete/error fields If logged in: banner "Your LO is currently working on your application"
5 LO edits fields, completes sections (often while on phone with borrower) Auto-save every 10 seconds, log all LO edits with timestamp Real-time updates visible if borrower logged in (read-only mode)
6 LO adds internal notes for file: "Completed income section via phone call" Store notes in audit trail, not visible to borrower -
7 LO clicks "Return to Borrower" or "Submit Application" If return: notify borrower to review and submit. If submit: process as LO-submitted. Email: "Your application has been updated. Please review and submit."
8 LO exits take-over mode Release application lock, log take-over duration and sections edited Regain edit access (if returned to borrower)

Concurrent Access & Locking Rules

Scenario System Behavior User Experience
LO takes over while borrower is idle Borrower session switches to read-only mode Borrower sees banner: "Your loan officer is currently working on your application. You can view but not edit."
LO takes over while borrower is actively editing Prompt LO: "Borrower is currently active. Take over anyway?" If yes, lock borrower out. Borrower sees modal: "[LO Name] needs to make some updates. Your session will resume when they're done."
Borrower tries to edit during LO take-over All input fields disabled, save button hidden Tooltip on disabled fields: "Your LO is updating this section. Refresh the page for latest changes."
LO exits take-over mode Send notification to borrower if logged in, re-enable edit mode Banner: "Your loan officer has finished updating your application. You can now continue."
Multiple LOs attempt take-over Only one LO can take over at a time. Second LO sees: "Application currently being edited by [LO Name]" No change (borrower only sees primary LO activity)
LO take-over times out (60 min idle) Auto-release lock, save progress, notify LO and borrower Email to borrower: "Your application is ready for you to review and continue."

Return/Submit Action: Whether LO returned to borrower or submitted directly
Notes: Any internal notes LO added for file
Borrower Communication: All notifications sent during take-over
