Project Overview

Vision & Mission

Smart App 2.0 represents a fundamental reimagining of the digital mortgage application experience. Our vision is to transform a traditionally complex, frustrating process into a streamlined, intelligent, and empowering journey that guides borrowers seamlessly from initial interest to loan approval. By leveraging cutting-edge technology integrations, AI-powered assistance, and research-backed UX patterns, we aim to increase application completion rates by 134%, reduce processing time by 56%, and dramatically improve both borrower and Loan Officer satisfaction.

Strategic Objectives

Borrower Experience Excellence

Achieve 78%+ application completion rate through progressive disclosure, instant identity verification, and intelligent data prefill. Reduce average time-to-complete from 42 minutes to 18.6 minutes.

Loan Officer Productivity

Increase LO productivity by 42% through real-time borrower monitoring, automated data collection, and intelligent intervention tools. Reduce manual data entry by 67%.

Operational Efficiency

Reduce processing time by 56% and operational costs by 34% through automation, third-party integrations, and streamlined workflows. Achieve 670% ROI within first year.

Technology Innovation

Establish platform as industry leader through AI assistance, instant verification, seamless integrations (Prove, Plaid, TRUV), and mobile-first responsive design.

Risk Register: Top 5 Risks

Identified risks with probability, impact assessment, and mitigation strategies actively monitored throughout project lifecycle.

HIGH RISK

Third-Party Integration Delays

Prove, Plaid, or TRUV integration complexity or API changes could delay development timeline by 2-4 weeks.

Impact
High
Mitigation Strategy:
Early engagement with vendor technical teams, parallel development of mock integrations, contingency plan for phased integration rollout if needed.
MEDIUM RISK

Scope Creep

Stakeholder requests for additional features beyond MVP could expand scope and delay launch timeline.

Impact
Medium
Mitigation Strategy:
Strict change control process, MVP vs. V2 feature prioritization framework, regular stakeholder alignment on scope boundaries.
MEDIUM RISK

Compliance & Regulatory Requirements

New compliance requirements or regulatory changes during development could require significant rework.

Impact
Medium
Mitigation Strategy:
Weekly compliance team reviews, modular architecture for easier updates, proactive monitoring of regulatory landscape.
LOW RISK

Resource Availability

Key team member unavailability (illness, attrition) could temporarily slow progress in specialized areas.

Impact
Low
Mitigation Strategy:
Cross-training team members, comprehensive documentation, maintain bench of contractors for rapid augmentation if needed.
LOW RISK

User Adoption Challenges

Loan Officers may resist new workflows or technology, requiring extended training and change management.

Impact
Low
Mitigation Strategy:
Early LO involvement in design, comprehensive training program, phased rollout with champion LOs, ongoing support infrastructure.
