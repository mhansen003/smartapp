üö® ESCALATION PATHS

Clear escalation paths for different types of issues ensure rapid resolution and proper stakeholder involvement.

P0 Production Incident
  • 1. Page on-call engineer via PagerDuty
  • 2. Post in #smartapp-incidents
  • 3. Notify Director of Engineering (immediately)
  • 4. Escalate to VP of Product if customer-impacting
  • 5. Create war room if needed (>1 hour outage)
Technical Architecture Decision
  • 1. Discuss in Architecture Review meeting
  • 2. Create RFC document with pros/cons
  • 3. Review with Senior Engineers
  • 4. Get approval from Director of Engineering
  • 5. Document decision in team wiki
Product / Scope Changes
  • 1. Raise with Product Manager
  • 2. Assess impact on timeline and resources
  • 3. Review with Head of Product
  • 4. Get stakeholder approval if scope increase
  • 5. Update roadmap and communicate to team
Third-Party Integration Issues
  • 1. Check vendor status page
  • 2. Contact vendor support (Plaid/TRUV/Prove)
  • 3. Loop in Technical PM (Brandon Clark)
  • 4. Escalate to vendor Account Manager if >4 hours
  • 5. Implement fallback/workaround if critical
Security / Compliance Concerns
  • 1. Email security@company.com immediately
  • 2. Do NOT share details in public channels
  • 3. Notify Director of Engineering
  • 4. Follow incident response playbook
  • 5. Coordinate with Legal/Compliance teams
Team Conflicts / HR Issues
  • 1. Attempt to resolve directly if comfortable
  • 2. Discuss with your direct manager
  • 3. Involve Director/Head of Department
  • 4. Contact HR Business Partner if needed
  • 5. Anonymous reporting via ethics hotline available
Timeline / Resource Constraints
  • 1. Discuss with Technical PM / Scrum Master
  • 2. Raise in sprint planning or retro
  • 3. Escalate to Director of Engineering
  • 4. Review scope/timeline with Product
  • 5. Stakeholder communication on revised plan
Design / UX Disagreements
  • 1. Bring to Design Review meeting
  • 2. Share user research data/rationale
  • 3. Involve Lead Designer (David Kim)
  • 4. Consider A/B test if unable to align
  • 5. Head of Product makes final call
