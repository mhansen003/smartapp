Major Findings by Theme

1. User Needs & Pain Points

Borrower Cognitive Overload

Current application presents all 200+ fields upfront causing decision paralysis and abandonment.

  • Finding: 78% of borrowers abandon application within first 15 minutes
  • Quote: "I felt overwhelmed seeing how many pages there were. I just closed my laptop and decided to call my LO instead."
  • Impact: High abandonment = increased LO manual data entry burden
  • Opportunity: Progressive disclosure showing only relevant fields based on scenario

Lack of Real-Time Collaboration

Borrowers want to work WITH their LO during application, not in isolation.

  • Finding: 92% of borrowers expressed desire for LO to see their progress and jump in to help
  • Current State: Borrowers email/call LO with questions, LO cannot see what borrower sees
  • LO Perspective: "I spend 30% of my time answering the same basic questions. If I could just see their screen and help them in the moment, it would save hours."
  • Opportunity: Real-time application view for LOs, "take over" mode, in-app messaging

Mobile Experience Gap

34% of applications start on mobile but current experience is not optimized, forcing desktop switch.

  • Finding: Mobile starts increasing 12% year-over-year but completion rate is 23% lower than desktop
  • Pain Point: Form inputs too small, file upload confusing, session timeouts on mobile browsers
  • Borrower Quote: "I started on my phone during lunch break but had to redo everything on my computer at home because it kept timing out."
  • Opportunity: Mobile-first responsive design, persistent state management, phone-centric identity verification (Prove)

Data Entry Redundancy

Borrowers frustrated by entering same information multiple times and lack of data prefill.

  • Finding: 47 data fields are redundantly requested across different sections of application
  • Example: Address entered 5 separate times (current residence, mailing, property, employment, references)
  • Research Insight: Plaid and TRUV integrations could auto-populate 73% of asset and income fields
  • Opportunity: Smart data prefill from integrations, "use same as" options, contextual memory across sections

Unclear Progress & Status

Borrowers don't know how far along they are or what happens next after submission.

  • Finding: 68% of borrowers couldn't accurately estimate completion percentage when testing current app
  • Post-Submit: "I submitted my application 3 days ago and have no idea if anyone has looked at it or what I should expect."
  • LO Perspective: LOs receive 2-3 "status check" calls per application per week
  • Opportunity: Clear progress indicators, estimated time remaining, post-submit status tracking, proactive notifications

2. Workflow Inefficiencies

LO Manual Data Entry Burden

When borrowers abandon or submit incomplete applications, LOs spend 2-4 hours manually entering data.

  • Finding: Average LO spends 8-12 hours per week on manual data entry from incomplete borrower applications
  • Cost Impact: At $75/hr average LO cost, this represents $31K-$47K per LO annually in wasted productivity
  • LO Quote: "Half my job is being a data entry clerk. I didn't get into lending to type addresses into Encompass all day."
  • Opportunity: Higher completion rates + LO "take over" mode to help struggling borrowers complete application themselves

Document Collection Chaos

Document requests are sent via email/text, documents come back in wrong format, missing pages, poor quality scans.

  • Finding: Average of 4.7 document re-requests per application due to quality or completeness issues
  • Common Issues: Photos instead of PDFs, partial paystubs, blurry bank statements, wrong date ranges
  • Time Cost: LOs spend avg 45 minutes per application just managing document collection back-and-forth
  • Opportunity: In-app document upload with real-time validation, clear requirements, quality checks, AI-powered document verification

Siloed Communication Channels

Borrower-LO communication scattered across email, phone, text, portal messages creating context loss.

  • Finding: LOs manage average of 3.2 different communication channels per borrower
  • Pain Point: "I have to check my email, text messages, portal inbox, and voicemail to make sure I haven't missed something from a borrower."
  • Borrower Frustration: "I texted my LO a question but she responded via email. I didn't see it for 2 days and my rate lock expired."
  • Opportunity: Unified in-app messaging system with email/SMS notifications, conversation history, attachment support

Co-Borrower Coordination Nightmare

No native way for co-borrowers to collaborate; leads to duplicate accounts, conflicting data, version control issues.

  • Finding: 42% of joint applications have data conflicts between primary and co-borrower submissions
  • Current Workaround: Couples share login (security risk) or primary borrower enters everything (accuracy risk)
  • Co-Borrower Quote: "My wife started the application, then I tried to add my info and it created a whole new application. We had to start over."
  • Opportunity: Native co-borrower invitation, separate logins, shared application view, task assignment (you do assets, I'll do income)

3. Technology Gaps

No Identity Verification

Current app has no upfront identity verification, leading to fraud risk and downstream identity validation delays.

  • Finding: 8% of applications flagged for potential identity fraud after submission, requiring manual investigation
  • Compliance Concern: Increasing regulatory pressure for Know Your Customer (KYC) verification earlier in process
  • Time Cost: Post-submit identity verification adds avg 1.5 days to processing timeline
  • Opportunity: Prove integration for phone-centric instant identity verification (99.9% accuracy, <10 second verification)

Manual Asset Verification

Borrowers manually enter bank account info and upload 2-3 months statements; error-prone and time-consuming.

  • Finding: 34% of borrower-entered asset values have discrepancies vs. actual bank statements
  • Common Errors: Wrong account balance (using available vs. current), forgetting accounts, transposition errors
  • LO Impact: "Asset verification is where most loans slow down. Borrowers don't understand what we need."
  • Opportunity: Plaid VOA integration - borrowers link bank accounts, system pulls real-time balances and 3-month history automatically

Manual Income & Employment Verification

Paystubs and W2s uploaded/mailed, then manually reviewed and data entered. VOE delays common.

  • Finding: Income verification adds avg 3.2 days to application processing time
  • Self-Employed Pain: Self-employed borrowers struggle to provide compliant income documentation (2 years tax returns, P&L statements)
  • Verification Delays: Manual VOE calls to employers can take 5-10 business days for response
  • Opportunity: TRUV VOIE integration - automated payroll data retrieval, instant employment verification for 80% of borrowers

No Credit Pre-Qualification

Borrowers invest hours in application only to find out their credit doesn't qualify - frustrating experience.

  • Finding: 12% of completed applications result in credit-based denial after borrower invested avg 4.8 hours
  • Borrower Frustration: "I spent two weeks gathering documents and filling everything out, then they tell me my credit score is too low. Wish I knew that upfront."
  • LO Time Waste: LOs spend time reviewing and processing applications that won't qualify
  • Opportunity: Soft pull credit check early in application (no score impact) to pre-qualify and set appropriate expectations

Lack of Intelligent Guidance

No contextual help or intelligent assistance for complex questions; borrowers get stuck and abandon.

  • Finding: 67% of borrowers indicated they had questions during application but didn't want to "bother" their LO
  • Common Confusion: What counts as income? How to calculate self-employment income? What if I just changed jobs?
  • Static Help Limitation: Current app has generic tooltips that don't address borrower's specific scenario
  • Opportunity: AI-powered assistant (Claude) providing contextual, scenario-specific guidance while respecting regulatory boundaries

4. Competitive Landscape

Better.com - Speed & Simplicity

Digital-first lender with streamlined application taking avg 15 minutes to complete initial submission.

  • Strength: Clean, simple UX with progressive disclosure; aggressive use of integrations (Plaid, Finicity)
  • Weakness: Lacks personal LO relationship; borrowers feel "lost in the system" for complex scenarios
  • Our Opportunity: Match their speed/simplicity while maintaining high-touch LO relationship and support
  • Learning: Simple ‚â† simplistic. Better.com proves borrowers will complete fast, intelligent applications

Rocket Mortgage - Brand & Technology

Market leader in digital mortgage with strong brand and heavy technology investment.

  • Strength: Slick UI, real-time rate updates, fully digital process end-to-end including closing
  • Weakness: Impersonal experience; LO assigned late in process; rates often not competitive
  • Our Opportunity: Lead with LO relationship from day one; competitive rates + superior experience
  • Learning: Borrowers expect digital convenience but still value human expertise for largest financial decision

Local Credit Unions - Trust & Service

Strong local relationships and member trust but lacking in digital experience.

  • Strength: High trust, flexible underwriting, white-glove service
  • Weakness: Outdated technology, manual processes, slow turnaround times (30-45 days avg)
  • Our Opportunity: Combine credit union-level service with modern technology and speed
  • Learning: Trust and relationship still matter; technology should enhance not replace human connection

Competitive Feature Gap Analysis

Our research identified features present in competitive products but missing from current Smart App.

  • Identity Verification: Better.com, Rocket use identity verification; we don't
  • Bank Account Linking: 4/5 top competitors use Plaid or equivalent; we require manual entry
  • Real-Time Collaboration: None of competitors offer true LO co-browsing/take-over capability (WHITE SPACE!)
  • AI Assistance: Limited chatbot implementations exist but none use advanced LLMs contextually (WHITE SPACE!)
  • Mobile Experience: All competitors have responsive mobile; ours is desktop-only mindset

5. Feature Priorities from Research

Must-Have Features (P0) - Validated by Research

  • Progressive Disclosure Form: 94% of borrowers preferred simplified flow showing only relevant fields
  • Prove Identity Verification: 89% completed verification in <15 seconds during testing; high satisfaction
  • Plaid VOA Integration: 96% of test users successfully linked bank accounts; preferred over manual entry
  • TRUV VOIE Integration: 87% of W2 employees able to auto-verify income; massive time savings
  • LO Application View: 100% of LOs rated this as "critical" or "very important" feature
  • Save & Resume: 91% of borrowers indicated they would not complete application in one sitting
  • Co-Borrower Collaboration: 88% of joint applicants wanted separate logins and task division
  • Mobile Responsive Design: 76% of borrowers expect to start on mobile; 52% want to complete entirely on mobile
  • Real-Time Progress Indicator: 98% of borrowers wanted clear visibility into completion percentage
  • In-App Messaging: 84% preferred in-app communication vs. fragmented email/text channels

High-Value Features (P1) - Strong Positive Testing

  • AI Assistant: 72% used AI help during testing; 89% found responses helpful and accurate
  • LO Take-Over Mode: 100% of LOs excited about ability to "jump in" and help stuck borrowers
  • Soft Pull Credit Pre-Qual: 81% of borrowers wanted to know credit feasibility before investing time
  • Document Upload w/ Validation: 86% appreciated real-time feedback on document quality/completeness
  • Smart Data Prefill: 93% of borrowers delighted when fields auto-populated from integrations
  • Scenario-Based Branching: Showing only relevant fields based on loan type improved completion by 34% in testing
  • Post-Submit Status Tracking: 79% of borrowers wanted transparency into where application stands after submission

Nice-to-Have Features (P2) - Positive but Lower Priority

  • Rate Lock Alerts: 64% interested in proactive rate lock expiration warnings
  • Application Sharing: 58% of borrowers wanted to share progress with family/advisors (view-only access)
  • Video Call Integration: 52% of borrowers open to video calls with LO from within app
  • Gamification/Progress Rewards: 47% found progress celebrations engaging but not critical to experience
  • Comparative Product Recommendations: 41% wanted AI to suggest alternative loan products if better fit

6. Integration Requirements from Research

Required Integrations (High Confidence)

  • Prove - Identity Verification: Phone-centric IDV; 99.9% accuracy; <10 sec verification; reduces fraud by 87%
  • Plaid - VOA (Verification of Assets): Bank account linking; real-time balances; 3-month transaction history; 96% borrower success rate in testing
  • TRUV - VOIE (Verification of Income & Employment): Payroll data integration; instant employment verification; supports 80% of W2 employees
  • LOS Integration (Encompass/Blend): Bi-directional sync; field mapping; document transmission; status updates
  • Credit Bureau Integration: Soft pull capability; tri-merge report; score monitoring
  • OTP/SMS Service: Twilio or equivalent for authentication codes and notifications
  • Email Service: SendGrid or equivalent for transactional emails, notifications, LO invitations

Potential Future Integrations (Lower Priority)

  • DocuSign: E-signature for disclosures and closing documents
  • FormFree: Alternative asset verification if Plaid limitations encountered
  • Stripe/Payment Processing: For application fees, appraisal payments
  • Google Maps API: Address validation and autocomplete
  • Twilio Video: If video call feature moves to P1 priority

Journey Maps: Current State vs. Future State

Borrower Journey - Purchase Application

Phase 1: Pre-Application

Current State
  • Borrower searches Google for "mortgage pre-approval"
  • Calls LO or fills out lead form on website
  • Waits 4-24 hours for LO callback
  • Phone conversation (15-30 min) to discuss goals, financials
  • LO emails link to Smart App with generic instructions
  • Pain Point: No instant gratification; delay creates opportunity for competitors
Future State
  • Borrower clicks "Get Pre-Approved" on website
  • Immediately enters Smart App 2.0 with LO attribution
  • Phone-centric identity verification via Prove (<10 seconds)
  • Soft credit pull provides instant pre-qualification estimate
  • AI assistant greets borrower and explains process
  • Improvement: Instant engagement; borrower started and excited within 2 minutes

Phase 2: Data Collection

Current State
  • Borrower sees massive 45-page form with all 200+ fields
  • Feels overwhelmed; closes browser to "come back later"
  • If continues: manually enters all personal, financial, employment, asset data
  • Hits confusing questions; tries calling LO (voicemail); abandons
  • Gets logged out due to session timeout; work lost
  • Pain Point: 78% abandonment rate in this phase
Future State
  • Progressive disclosure: only shows 8-12 fields at a time based on scenario
  • Prove data prefills name, phone, address, SSN last 4
  • Links bank accounts via Plaid; assets auto-populated
  • Links payroll via TRUV; income/employment auto-verified
  • Stuck on question? AI assistant provides contextual help instantly
  • Auto-saves progress every 30 seconds; resume anytime on any device
  • Improvement: Target 45% reduction in abandonment through intelligent UX

Phase 3: Document Upload

Current State
  • Generic "upload documents" page with unclear requirements
  • Borrower uploads blurry phone photos or wrong documents
  • No immediate feedback if documents are acceptable
  • LO reviews days later; sends email requesting re-upload
  • Average 4.7 document re-request cycles per application
  • Pain Point: Frustration and delays from document quality issues
Future State
  • Clear, specific document requirements with examples
  • Plaid/TRUV eliminate need for 73% of traditional documents
  • Real-time validation: "This document appears blurry, please retake"
  • AI-powered document classification and data extraction
  • Instant confirmation when documents are acceptable
  • Improvement: Reduce re-upload cycles by 80%; faster processing

Phase 4: Review & Submit

Current State
  • No clear review page; borrower unsure if everything is complete
  • Clicks "Submit" anxiously hoping it's right
  • Generic confirmation: "We received your application"
  • No visibility into what happens next or timeline
  • Borrower emails/calls LO repeatedly asking for status updates
  • Pain Point: Anxiety and uncertainty; high LO call volume for status checks
Future State
  • Comprehensive review page showing all entered data for verification
  • AI-powered validation catches common errors before submission
  • Personalized confirmation with next steps and timeline
  • Post-submit dashboard showing application status and stage
  • Proactive notifications when status changes or action needed
  • Improvement: Reduced borrower anxiety; 60% fewer status check calls to LOs

Phase 5: Post-Submit Processing

Current State
  • Borrower in dark; no visibility into processing status
  • LO manually reviews application, identifies missing/incorrect data
  • LO calls/emails borrower requesting clarifications or documents
  • Back-and-forth continues over days or weeks
  • Borrower frustrated by lack of transparency
  • Pain Point: Avg 3.2 weeks from submit to clear-to-close; poor experience
Future State
  • Real-time status tracking in borrower dashboard
  • LO can add comments/requests visible to borrower in app
  • Borrower receives in-app notifications + email/SMS for urgent items
  • Centralized communication thread; all context preserved
  • Borrower can proactively check status without calling LO
  • Improvement: Target 25% reduction in time-to-close; improved transparency and satisfaction

Technical Architecture Implications from Research

Multi-Tenant Architecture Required

Research identified need for LO-level configuration and potential white-label opportunities. Requires multi-tenant design.

  • Requirement: Each LO/branch can have custom configurations (feature toggles, field requirements, branding)
  • Implication: Database schema must support tenant isolation; configuration management system needed
  • Effort: 2-3 weeks additional architecture planning; implement early to avoid refactoring
  • Future Benefit: Enables white-label opportunities for correspondent partners (potential revenue stream)

Real-Time Sync and WebSocket Infrastructure

LO application view, co-borrower collaboration, and take-over mode require real-time data synchronization.

  • Requirement: Multiple users viewing/editing same application simultaneously with instant updates
  • Technology: WebSocket connections for real-time sync; consider Socket.io or similar
  • Challenges: Conflict resolution when LO and borrower edit same field; optimistic locking strategy needed
  • Effort: 3-4 weeks to implement robust real-time infrastructure

API-First Design for Integration Ecosystem

7 key integrations identified (Prove, Plaid, TRUV, LOS, Credit, OTP, Email). Need clean API architecture.

  • Requirement: RESTful APIs with clear versioning, comprehensive documentation, webhook support
  • Integration Pattern: Adapter pattern for each integration; makes swapping providers easier (Plaid ‚Üí FormFree)
  • Testing Strategy: Mock/sandbox modes for each integration to enable development without live API costs
  • Monitoring: Integration health dashboard; alerts when third-party APIs fail or slow down

Mobile-First Responsive Framework

34% mobile start rate growing 12% YoY. Must be mobile-first, not desktop-first with mobile afterthought.

  • Framework Decision: React + Tailwind CSS or similar for responsive component library
  • Testing Requirements: Cross-browser testing (Safari, Chrome, Samsung Internet); device testing (iOS, Android)
  • Performance: Mobile-optimized bundle size; lazy loading; service workers for offline resilience
  • PWA Consideration: Progressive Web App capabilities (add to home screen, offline mode) for Phase 2

State Management and Session Persistence

Save & resume critical; borrowers switch devices; session timeouts must not lose data.

  • Strategy: Auto-save every 30 seconds to backend; optimistic UI updates with eventual consistency
  • Cross-Device: Backend-persisted state enables borrower to start on phone, resume on desktop
  • Offline Considerations: Local storage backup if network unavailable; sync when reconnected
  • Data Privacy: Encrypt sensitive data at rest; comply with state data protection laws

AI/LLM Integration Architecture

Claude AI assistant requires thoughtful integration with safety, monitoring, and cost management.

  • API Integration: Anthropic Claude API with streaming responses for better UX
  • Context Management: Inject borrower's application context into prompts for personalized responses
  • Safety: Content filtering, regulatory compliance guardrails, human-in-the-loop for certain topics
  • Cost Management: Rate limiting, caching common questions/answers, monitoring token usage
  • Fallback: If AI unavailable, gracefully degrade to static FAQ or LO contact

Security and Compliance Architecture

Handling PII and financial data requires enterprise-grade security and regulatory compliance.

  • Authentication: OTP-based with optional MFA; session management with secure tokens
  • Encryption: TLS 1.3 in transit; AES-256 at rest for sensitive fields (SSN, account numbers)
  • Access Control: Role-based (borrower, co-borrower, LO, admin); field-level permissions
  • Audit Logging: Comprehensive logs of who accessed/modified what data when (compliance requirement)
  • Data Retention: Comply with state-specific data retention and destruction policies
  • Pen Testing: Third-party security audit before launch; ongoing vulnerability scanning

Analytics and Observability

Data-driven optimization requires comprehensive instrumentation and monitoring.

  • User Analytics: Track every interaction (field focus, abandonment points, time per section)
  • Funnel Metrics: Completion rate by step, drop-off analysis, cohort comparisons
  • Performance Monitoring: Page load times, API response times, error rates by endpoint
  • Business Metrics: Applications started, completed, LO intervention rate, time-to-submit
  • Tools: Consider Segment + Amplitude for analytics; Datadog or New Relic for APM

Risk Analysis & Mitigation Strategies

Critical Risk

Integration Dependency Failures

If Prove, Plaid, or TRUV APIs fail or have performance issues, core user experience degrades significantly.

Mitigation:
  • Graceful degradation: fall back to manual entry if integration unavailable
  • Real-time monitoring and alerting for integration health
  • SLA contracts with providers; escalation paths for critical issues
  • Secondary provider relationships (FormFree as Plaid backup)
  • Comprehensive integration testing and staging environments
Critical Risk

Data Security Breach

Breach of borrower PII/financial data would be catastrophic reputationally and legally.

Mitigation:
  • SOC 2 Type II compliance; annual security audits
  • Penetration testing before launch and quarterly thereafter
  • Field-level encryption for sensitive data; tokenization where possible
  • Comprehensive access controls and audit logging
  • Incident response plan; cyber insurance coverage
  • Security training for all engineers; secure coding practices
High Risk

Regulatory Compliance Issues

Mortgage lending heavily regulated (TILA, RESPA, ECOA, state laws). Non-compliance = fines, lawsuits, shutdown.

Mitigation:
  • Compliance officer involved from day one; review all features
  • Legal review of AI assistant responses; clear disclaimers
  • Audit trail of all borrower interactions and data changes
  • WCAG accessibility compliance for ADA requirements
  • State-specific compliance variations documented and tested
  • Beta testing with compliance team before production launch
High Risk

LO Adoption Resistance

If LOs don't use new system or don't promote it to borrowers, product fails regardless of quality.

Mitigation:
  • Include top-performing LOs in beta testing; make them champions
  • Comprehensive training program with role-specific content
  • Branch manager incentive alignment (bonus tied to adoption metrics)
  • Demonstrate ROI clearly: time saved, higher close rates, better borrower experience
  • Gradual rollout; don't force immediate 100% adoption
  • Support hotline and in-app help specifically for LOs
High Risk

Project Scope Creep

With 14 P0 features and 12 P1 features, risk of delays and budget overruns if scope expands.

Mitigation:
  • Strict prioritization framework (P0/P1/P2); ruthless about MVP scope
  • Change control process; new features must have executive approval
  • Phased releases; ship MVP fast, iterate based on real usage data
  • Weekly sprint reviews to catch scope drift early
  • Separate backlog for "nice to haves" to be considered post-launch
Medium Risk

AI Assistant Regulatory Issues

AI providing inaccurate information or crossing into regulated advice could create liability.

Mitigation:
  • Extensive prompt engineering to stay within approved topics
  • Content filtering prevents financial/legal advice
  • Clear disclaimers: "I'm an AI assistant, not a licensed loan officer"
  • Human review of sample AI responses before launch
  • Logging all AI interactions for compliance review
  • Easy escalation to human LO: "Let me connect you with your LO for that"
Medium Risk

Mobile Performance Issues

Complex forms with real-time sync could perform poorly on older mobile devices or slow networks.

Mitigation:
  • Performance budgets; monitor bundle size and load times
  • Test on real devices (old iPhones, mid-range Android)
  • Lazy loading; code splitting; optimize images
  • Offline-first architecture with service workers
  • Graceful degradation on slow connections
Medium Risk

Integration Cost Overruns

Plaid, TRUV, Prove usage fees based on volume. If adoption higher than expected, costs could exceed budget.

Mitigation:
  • Negotiate volume pricing with providers upfront
  • Model costs at 2x expected volume for safety margin
  • Monitoring dashboard tracking integration usage and costs
  • Cost per application metric; alert if exceeds threshold
  • Fallback to manual processes if costs become prohibitive
