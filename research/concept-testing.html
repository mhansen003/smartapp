Concept Testing: Plaid Bank Connection Flow

Overview

The Plaid integration allows borrowers to connect their bank accounts for automated asset verification. This flow was tested extensively across all three rounds with iterative improvements based on user feedback.

Metric V1 V2 V3 Change
Task Success Rate 78% 84% 89% +11%
Average Completion Time 4.8 min 3.9 min 3.4 min -1.4 min
Confidence Score (1-5) 3.2 3.9 4.2 +1.0
Users Who Understood Data Shared 64% 81% 93% +29%

Key Findings

Positive Findings

  • Familiarity with Plaid: 68% of participants had used Plaid before (Venmo, Cash App, etc.) and felt comfortable with the flow
  • Speed Appreciation: Users consistently praised the speed compared to manual document upload: "This is so much faster than what I did with my last mortgage" (P14, 38F)
  • Bank Search: 89% found their bank quickly using the search in V3 (up from 71% in V1)
  • Visual Trust Indicators: Lock icons and "Bank-level encryption" messaging increased trust by 23% (measured via survey)

Issues & Friction Points

  • Security Concerns (V1): 36% expressed hesitation about sharing bank credentials. Resolved in V2 with enhanced trust messaging and "How is my data protected?" expandable section
  • Multi-Bank Confusion: Users with multiple banks (42% of sample) were initially unclear if they needed to connect all accounts or just primary checking. Clarified with inline help text in V2
  • Connection Failures: 11% experienced connection errors (bank timeouts, incorrect credentials). Error messaging improved in V3 with clearer recovery steps
  • Small Credit Unions: 8% of participants couldn't find their small local credit union in Plaid's directory, causing frustration. Added fallback "Manual upload" option prominently

Concept Testing: TRUV Employment Verification Flow

Overview

TRUV enables automated income and employment verification by connecting directly to payroll systems. This was a less familiar concept for users compared to Plaid, requiring more education and trust-building.

Metric V1 V2 V3 Change
Task Success Rate 71% 79% 85% +14%
Average Completion Time 6.2 min 5.1 min 4.2 min -2.0 min
Confidence Score (1-5) 2.9 3.5 3.9 +1.0
Found Employer Successfully 73% 81% 88% +15%

Key Findings

Positive Findings

  • Value Recognized: Once explained, 91% saw the value: "I'd rather do this than get pay stubs from HR" (P27)
  • Employer Coverage: 88% found their employer in the system (V3), up from 73% in V1 with improved search
  • Auto-fill Delight: Seeing income data auto-populate generated strong positive reactions

Issues & Friction Points

  • Unfamiliarity: 82% had never heard of TRUV, requiring more upfront explanation than Plaid
  • Payroll Login Confusion: 34% didn't know their payroll system login (ADP, Paychex, etc.). Added "I don't know my login" help option in V2
  • Multiple Jobs: 19% had multiple jobs/income sources and were unclear if they should verify all. Clarified in V3 with better instructions
  • Self-Employed Users: 12% were self-employed and felt frustrated this option didn't apply. Added clear fallback path in V2

Concept Testing: OTP Authentication

Summary & Key Takeaways

Executive Summary

The Smart App 2.0 concept testing program successfully validated the core digital mortgage application experience through three rounds of iterative testing with 42 participants over 10 weeks. The final V3 prototype achieved all target usability metrics and demonstrated strong user acceptance across key features.

Key Successes

  • 91% task completion rate - Exceeded 85% target
  • 81.2 System Usability Scale (SUS) - "Good" to "Excellent" rating
  • +42 Net Promoter Score - Strong recommendation likelihood
  • 24% reduction in completion time - From 28.4 to 21.7 minutes across iterations
  • 56% reduction in errors - From 3.2 to 1.4 errors per session
  • High feature adoption - 93% would use Plaid, 88% would use AI assistant

Most Valuable Features (User Rankings)

  1. Plaid Bank Connection - Highest value, saves time on document gathering
  2. AI Assistant - High delight factor, instant help without LO call
  3. Save & Resume - Critical for busy borrowers completing in multiple sessions
  4. Prove Identity - Quick and seamless, sets positive first impression
  5. TRUV Employment Verification - Valuable but less familiar than Plaid

Remaining Challenges

  • Mobile experience - Takes 24-37% longer, needs progressive disclosure improvements
  • TRUV employer coverage - 12% couldn't find small employers
  • Self-employment workflow - Needs dedicated path for non-W2 income
  • Co-borrower addition timing - 31% had to backtrack (improved in V3 but not perfect)

Readiness for Beta Launch

Based on V3 results, Smart App 2.0 is ready for controlled beta testing with real borrowers and LO partners. All critical usability targets have been met, and the iterative testing process successfully identified and resolved major friction points. The recommended beta launch in Q1 2025 should proceed with:

  • Phased rollout starting with 3-5 pilot LOs and tech-savvy borrowers
  • Close monitoring of completion rates, data accuracy, and LO satisfaction
  • Continued iteration based on real-world usage patterns and edge cases
  • Priority focus on mobile experience improvements and TRUV employer coverage

Overall Assessment

Smart App 2.0 represents a significant improvement over traditional mortgage application experiences. The combination of automated verification (Plaid, TRUV, Prove), intelligent assistance (AI), and user-centered design creates a modern, efficient borrower experience that aligns with user expectations set by other digital financial services. With the recommended P0 and P1 improvements implemented, the application is well-positioned for successful market adoption.
